The format at a glance

Fife against Angus, and no running points ladder this year — every day stands alone with its own winner. Take 3 of the 5 days to win the week, and the individual net race runs underneath it all:
Better Ball pairs at Kingsbarns (with your father or son) and Dumbarnie (mixed pairings). Only your pair's lower net counts on each hole, so anyone can blow up without feeling bad. Two matches out there; the side that wins more takes the day.
4v4 at Carnoustie and the Old Course finale, the whole team walking together. Everyone plays their own ball and each side counts just its best two nets — the kindest game we have, on the hardest courses. Low team total takes the day.
Singles at the New Course. Everyone plays one on one against the one man they haven't shared a fairway with all week. Most match wins takes the day.

Teams

Two sides, two father-son pairings each. Locked for the week.

Groupings

The playing groups for each round. Different partner on each pairs day, whole team together on the 4v4 days, and everyone shares a fairway with everyone at least once over the week.

Rounds & results

Match play days: play it out, tap each winner — most wins takes the day. 4v4 days: enter each gross and the low best-2-nets team total takes the day.

Individual net leaderboard

The week's other trophy: every player ranked by cumulative net across every round they post a score. Low net over the five days is the overall individual champion.
